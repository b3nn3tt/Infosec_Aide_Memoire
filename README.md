# Introduction

I have been working in Information Security (or Cyber security if you are so inclined...) for over a decade, and in that time I have arrived at the conclusion that it is impossible for **most** mere mortals to commit to memory, and recall at will, everything we need (or are expected to know at the drop of a hat...) to do our jobs. There is simply too much that we, as InfoSec specialists, are expected to know off by heart.

Sadly, I was not blessed with eidetic memory, and I've never been fond of people who profess to know it all... In my experience, such folk seem incapable of acknowledging that either:

1. They **DONT** know it all (unfortunately very common) and there is some _**uniqueness**_** ** in their work...\

2. They are unique in their super human abilities, and that most of us do not possess such [phenomenal cosmic powers](https://64.media.tumblr.com/57299949cd82da34428e1cd50b803d85/tumblr\_inline\_ozqth2eCbZ1txdber\_1280.png)

Now of course, there are plenty of immensely talented, brilliant people out there who do not fall into the same category as the rest of us. I call them [Unicorns](https://i.pinimg.com/originals/9e/0e/dc/9e0edc3bf5589b737487f5680e2e9a46.gif), and perhaps one day we might `<joke>`capture one, study it, and replicate their powers for the greater good`</joke>`. However, in the meantime, I have to seek alternative options.

Before I go any further, I want to make a few things clear:

1. It is perfectly ok to say "_I don't know_" - it is **FAR** better to acknowledge a skills gap and do your research than it is to guess\

2. It is the norm to have to look things up continuously; few of us can remember it all\

3. It is better to know the core concepts; to know what is possible and look up the implementation, than remember commands without context

Those three points are why this Aide Memoire exists. Allow me to explain.

I am a firm believer that it is better to admit you don't know a thing than it is to blag it and f&\*k it all up. Often when I am working professionally, and I sense that not everyone in the room is on the same page, I will claim to not know what we are discussing so that we are forced to take a moment, as a collective, to ensure we are all up to speed. This saves someone else the "embarrassment" of saying the much dreaded phrase "I don't know", and is often met with a few relieved faces. My intent here is two fold:

1. Remove the apparent stigma surrounding the act of admittance\

2. Disempowering those whose confidence greatly outweighs their capabilities - I am quite opposed to the "those who speak loudest are correct" notion...

I also think that its better to learn how things work from a high-level perspective, then drill down into the specifics afterwards, than it is to blindly follow a process or guide with no real understanding of why things are happening. To do so is a sure fire way to develop bad practices, make poor business decisions, or introduce vulnerabilities. I'd rather be able to follow a concept, or understand a business requirement, and transform my understanding into sound implementation using continuously tested, verified, repeatable, documented methods.

So, I work on this Aide Memoire iteratively as I learn more, building an ever expanding knowledge base that I can refer to when I work. In doing so, I remove my reliance on committing arbitrary commands to memory. I enjoy writing my own guides and explanations for things that I can repeat as I find committing my understanding to paper helps iron out the kinks, ensuring consistency in my work - I can also follow my own advice when I'm feeling a little absent minded.

I only hope that it useful to you, the reader. So please, enjoy my ramblings.&#x20;

Let's get started.
