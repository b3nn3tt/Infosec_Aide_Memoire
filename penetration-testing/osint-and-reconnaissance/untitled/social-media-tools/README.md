# Social Media Tools

Social Media has dramatically changed how OSINT reconnaissance is performed today. In the past, you would perhaps have to resort to clever social engineering techniques, or dumpster diving, to find that snippet of information you need. Nowadays, a quick scrape over peoples social media accounts will usually give you everything you need to know.

To quote [Stewart Lee](https://www.stewartlee.co.uk/):

> " I hate _Twitter_. It's like a state surveillance agency run by _gullible volunteers_. A Stasi for the Angry Birds generation"

It's a quite apposite quote, I think. If people aren't telling you their favourite food, or remembering a beloved childhood pet on the 30th anniversary of their tragic passing, they are openly celebrating a birthday, or telling you their favourite Cure albums... in order... whether you asked them to or not... 

![Modern social engineering...](../../../../.gitbook/assets/image%20%2869%29.png)

The point is, people offer up a lot of information that they perhaps don't realise is absolute gold to a would-be attacker
