# OSINT & Reconnaissance

During penetration testing, elsewise known as ethical hacking, you will generally adhere to a methodology that will direct your activities. Sadly, you cant just sit there, bang on the keyboard in some incoherent high speed fashion and then shout "_I'm in!!_"... sorry Hollywood.&#x20;

![](<../../.gitbook/assets/image (98).png>)

Intelligence gathering, or Reconnaissance as it is called in certain circles, is usually the first step. It is the process of acquiring intelligence on a target that will aid in our attack. Generally speaking, we break down our reconnaissance into two categories:

* **Passive Reconnaissance**\
  Here, you acquire as much information as you can about your target without establishing any contact between yourself and the target system(s). The chances of being discovered are extremely low, since you will be mostly leveraging information that is publicly available. The act of scouring publicly available information is known as **Open Source Intelligence** (**OSINT**) gathering, and it is a substantial subset of the reconnaissance phase\

* **Active Reconnaissance**\
  Here, you acquire information by scanning, interaction, and enumeration, which therefore includes establishing contact with the target system(s). The chances of being discovered performing active reconnaissance are much higher than when conducting passive information gathering as you are sending tangible packets to your target. Depending on the type of engagement you are on, such as Red Team or ITHC, you may need to be careful to make too much noise on the network, else you will be caught

The importance of thorough reconnaissance is that it informs your attack strategy. We use the data found during the reconnaissance phase to help use make better targeting and attack decisions. Consequently,  is by far the most time-intensive phase of the engagement, as you will want to be aware of all the potential attack vectors that can be leveraged prior to any attempts at exploitation.
